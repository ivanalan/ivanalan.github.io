GT Grand Challenges Program

January 2018 - May 2019

My Freshman year I was accepted into a program at Georgia Tech called Grand Challenges. This class was designed to teach leadership and teamwork skills through completing a project that solved a “wicked problem” in the world that aligned with one of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals.

We were placed in groups based on our interest in different UN Sustainable Development Goals. Our 5 person team was formed due to our mutual interest in SDG #4: Quality Education.

After many months of proposing potential solutions, receiving feedback, and analyzing different areas of education we could enter, we pursued our final project presented to the left.

Our product is an app that directly addresses the issue of delayed gratification that may affect students later in life. Our prototype is an application for parents to limit the daily amount of phone screen time on their children's device. However, the child may choose to save some of that daily screen time and the longer it is held in a "savings account", the more interest it gains (more screen time that could be used later).

For example, say a child is limited to 5 hours a day of screen time. The child may then choose to save 1 hour of screen time on Monday. For every day, the amount saved gains interest of 5%. So then after a week, the child may choose to cash in their saved time and be allowed 1.5 hours of screen time.
